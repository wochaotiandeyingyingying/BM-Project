    A lithium ion battery is a secondary battery that relies mainly on lithium ions to move between a positive electrode and a negative electrode to operate. During charging and discharging, Li+ intercalates and deintercalates between the two electrodes: when charging, Li+ is deintercalated from the positive electrode, and the electrolyte is embedded in the negative electrode, and the negative electrode is in a lithium-rich state;
